      <p>
        While completing my master's, I had the opportunity to contribute to another research project with two co-authors under Dr. Ersotelos, Dr. Pantelaki, and Dr. Adeel at UWE. The research focused on developing a serious game to educate users about the importance of transport decarbonisation and achieving net-zero carbon emissions by 2050 — aligning with the UK's Paris Agreement commitments.
      </p>
      <p>
        The prototype models real-world vehicle emission data and offers eight policy levers, such as upgrading fleets to electric vehicles. Play-testing with university students and professionals showed that richer visuals, interactive tutorials, and immediate audio-visual feedback dramatically improved engagement, comprehension, and learning motivation.
      </p>
      <p><strong>My contributions to the serious game development:</strong></p>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Back-End Logic &amp; Simulation Loop:</strong> Implemented tracking of monthly CO2 emissions, token refilling, and number of vehicles on the road. Developed an algorithm to simulate the impact of different transportation strategies on carbon emissions.</li>
        <li><strong>Vehicle Movement System:</strong> Designed and implemented a vehicle movement system using Unity splines, ensuring smooth and realistic vehicle paths with distinct vehicle types.</li>
        <li><strong>User Interface:</strong> Developed a comprehensive UI displaying CO2 emissions, token counts, and vehicle statistics with interactive elements for strategic decisions.</li>
        <li><strong>Web Deployment:</strong> Built for WebGL and hosted on Itch.io, making it accessible through web browsers.</li>
      </ul>
